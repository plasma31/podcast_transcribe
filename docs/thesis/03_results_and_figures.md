# Chapter: Results — Model Comparison

> Comparison of the first family of topic models over the 191,183-chunk corpus.
> All numbers come from `outputs/bertopic_run_comparison_full_only.csv` and the
> per-run `run_config.json`; figures are generated by
> `docs/thesis/_make_stats_and_figures.py`.

## 1. Experimental grid

Eight comparable runs were trained on the identical chunk corpus (191,183
chunks, 4,400 episodes, 83 podcasts), varying the embedding model and the
clustering/representation parameters. Because the corpus and the chunking are
held constant, differences in outcome are attributable to the model settings.

(The modelled corpus is slightly smaller than the 4,530 episodes / 84 podcasts
registered in the manifest. One podcast had all of its episodes fail at the
transcription stage and so contributed nothing; among the 4,416 successfully
processed episodes, a further 16 produced no chunk meeting the 20-word minimum.
This leaves 4,400 episodes across 83 podcasts in the chunk corpus.)

| Run | Embedding | UMAP n_neighbors / n_comp | HDBSCAN mcs / ms | max_df | nr_topics | Train time |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `bertopic_minilm_n100_t200` | MiniLM | 100 / 5 | 50 / 1 | 0.95 | 200 | 24 min |
| `bertopic_full_n100_t150` | MiniLM | 100 / 5 | 50 / 1 | 0.95 | 150 | 91 min |
| `bertopic_full_mcs30` | MiniLM | 30 / 5 | 30 / 1 | 0.95 | — | 43 min |
| `bertopic` (baseline) | MiniLM | 30 / 5 | 50 / 1 | 0.95 | — | 43 min |
| `bertopic_mpnet_n100_t200` | mpnet | 100 / 5 | 50 / 1 | 0.95 | 200 | 53 min |
| `bertopic_minilm_n100_t200_ms5` | MiniLM | 100 / 5 | 50 / 5 | 0.95 | 200 | 24 min |
| `bertopic_e5_mcs50_ms1` | e5-large | 50 / 10 | 50 / 1 | 0.95 | — | 134 min |
| `bertopic_multilingual_e5_large` | e5-large | 50 / 10 | 80 / 5 | 0.85 | — | 134 min |

(`mcs` = `min_cluster_size`, `ms` = `min_samples`.)

## 2. Outcomes

| Run | Topics (excl. outlier) | Outlier rate | Assigned chunks |
|---|---|---|---|
| `bertopic_minilm_n100_t200` | 199 | **51.0 %** | 93,677 |
| `bertopic_full_n100_t150` | 149 | 52.3 % | 91,192 |
| `bertopic_full_mcs30` | 622 | 56.6 % | 82,964 |
| `bertopic` (baseline) | 370 | 57.5 % | 81,241 |
| `bertopic_mpnet_n100_t200` | 199 | 61.7 % | 73,203 |
| `bertopic_minilm_n100_t200_ms5` | 199 | 64.5 % | 67,844 |
| `bertopic_e5_mcs50_ms1` | 315 | 67.8 % | 61,488 |
| `bertopic_multilingual_e5_large` | 145 | 71.7 % | 54,185 |

### Reading the comparison

- **Embedding model.** Counter to the intuition that a larger model is better,
  the lightweight **MiniLM produced the lowest outlier rates** (51–58 %), while
  `e5-large` produced the **highest** (68–72 %) on this corpus. The stronger
  model spreads chunks more finely in embedding space, which — at a fixed
  `min_cluster_size` — pushes more of them below the density threshold and into
  the outlier class. MiniLM is therefore both cheaper (24–43 min vs. 134 min)
  and, here, more inclusive.
- **`min_cluster_size`.** Lowering it from 50 to 30 (`full_mcs30`) raised the
  topic count sharply (370 → 622) by admitting many small clusters, at a slight
  cost in outlier rate. It is the primary lever on topic granularity.
- **`min_samples`.** Raising it from 1 to 5 (`minilm…_ms5`) made clustering more
  conservative and pushed the outlier rate up (51 % → 64.5 %) for the same
  topic count — confirming the choice of `min_samples = 1` in the baseline.
- **`nr_topics`.** The `_t200` / `_t150` runs merge topics post-hoc to a fixed
  target (200 / 150), trading granularity for a cleaner, more reportable set; it
  does not by itself reduce outliers.

There is a genuine **coverage–granularity–purity trade-off** rather than a single
best run: `minilm_n100_t200` maximises coverage and is the most defensible
"headline" model, while the unsuffixed baseline favours data-driven granularity
(370 topics, no forced merge) and is used for the structural analysis below.

## 3. Baseline topic structure

The baseline run yields **370 topics** (plus the outlier class). Topic sizes are
strongly right-skewed: a few large themes and a long tail of specific ones
(median topic size 108 chunks; largest 2,447).

![Baseline topic-size distribution](figures/fig_baseline_topic_size.png)

The largest topics are coherent and substantively interpretable — the leading
themes include religion/church, school and education, parenting/children,
masculinity and gender, democracy and party politics, care and patients, media
and journalism, housing, and mental health/therapy:

![Baseline: 20 largest topics](figures/fig_baseline_top_topics.png)

Each topic's full label is the top c-TF-IDF terms; for example topic 0 is
`kirche · religion · katholischen · katholische`, topic 2 is
`schule · lehrer · bildung · schulen`, and topic 5 is
`männer · männern · männlichkeit · frauenhaus`. The coherence of these labels —
together with the successful suppression of personal names — indicates the
embedding + c-TF-IDF combination recovers meaningful thematic structure from
spontaneous German speech.

## 4. Reproducing the figures

```bash
.venv/bin/python docs/thesis/_make_stats_and_figures.py
```

This reads only the committed pipeline outputs (`outputs/state/manifest.parquet`,
`outputs/parquet/episodes/*`, the baseline run, and the comparison CSV), writes
the eight PNGs under `docs/thesis/figures/`, and refreshes
`docs/thesis/corpus_stats.json`. Cross-run tables can be regenerated with
`pipeline/compare_bertopic_runs.py`, which scans every
`outputs/bertopic*/podcast_chunks_sw-de/` directory.
